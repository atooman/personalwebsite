Who am I?
I am a rising senior at the University of Michigan studying Computer Science in the College of Engineering with a minor in User Experience Design in the School of Information. I am from Cincinnati, Ohio and attended high school at Ursuline Academy. Raised a wolverine in enemy territory, it has been a dream to be able to attend one of the top public institutions in the nation.

Why computer science?
I intially chose computer science because of the creativity of the field. I took an introductory class in high school dedicated to app developement and fell in love with the idea of constantly being able to innovate and enhance the features of an application I created. As a student at the University of Michigan, I have been exposed to a much more technical side of computer science. Just as I fell in love with computer science for its creativity, I also enjoy it for its emphasis on problem solving and the thrill of finally fixing a bug in my program.
What am I passionate about?
My newest passion is finding new passions. The most recent hobby I have been exploring is crocheting, and I am excited to start piano lessons when I return to Michigan in the fall. I also enjoy spending time outdoors whether that be through hiking, swimming, or playing sports. In my professional life, I am passionate about using my talent and skills in order to make a positive difference in the world.
Kappa Theta Pi
Kappa Theta Pi is Michigan's premier professional technology fraternity with members across various disciplines united by a fervor for technology. Our fraternity's core revolves around five pillars: fostering professional growth, nurturing alumni connections, facilitating social expansion, driving technological progress, and providing academic support. Our commitment to these principles is evident through our engagement in project teams, study groups, professional development workshops, and hackathons/design jams. Within KTP, I have participated in the ethical, mobile, and design committees as well as been part of the team that created a mobile application to streamline the rush process.
Michigan Daily
I have been part of the Michigan Daily web developement team for one semester. In that time I worked on creating a new page under the style guide. The style guide is an internal site that can be utilized by designers on the Michigan Daily team in order to download and reference standard font styles, colors, and formats. I worked specifically on creating the page with fonts so that designers can see different fonts utilized on the Daily. To create this webpage, I was first given a Figma page which I then recreated using HTML, CSS, and React elements.
Miss STEMS Career
Miss Stems Careers is an organization dedicated to sparking early interest in STEM careers among young girls. I led the design and execution of a dynamic computer science workshop tailored specifically for middle school girls. This workshop was strategically crafted to captivate their curiosity and kindle an early fascination with the world of technology. Through interactive activities, hands-on experiences, and engaging discussions, participants were introduced to the captivating possibilities within the technology sector. By cultivating an environment of creativity and empowerment, Miss STEMS Careers is shaping the future by inspiring the next generation of female technology leaders.
